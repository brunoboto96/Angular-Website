MEF


We are MEF, Mary Etty Foundation.
The vision of MEF is to provide interim housing of up to one year with skill training so that clients become empowered, independent individuals. Transitional Housing's services are unique in their female-only clientele, long-duration transitional housing, and comprehensive empowerment program. Transitional Housing will make a significant impact on the Home Counties and for DV victims and its surrounding community, in both as a short-term relief, and as a long-term skill development and empowerment facility.

What we offer

The assistance program provides each client with a tailor made individual design program that teaches them necessary life skills and alongside counseling to help put the lives of the women back together. The collaborative approach in each program is to ultimately empower the women which is the keystone to developing self-sufficiency in each client. The steps include to name a few, personal development, vocational training, job assistance, assistance in seeking future housing, assistance in educational qualification, assistance in attending substance abuse counseling, emotionally and family counseling, interpersonal skills and independent living skills and self-awareness of safety for both the mother and the child.
